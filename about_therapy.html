ABOUT THERAPY AND SESSIONS


When working with younger children I usually meet with parents first to enable them to speak freely and in confidence. I then meet the child for one or two sessions, often with a parent present for some of the appointment time. If relevant, with parental permission, I will liaise with the school and other professionals. Following this, I will meet with the parents again to discuss a plan, which may include other specialist assessments. This process may vary with adolescents, who can be sensitive to parental involvement, which we would discuss in an initial phone conversation. These initial sessions are not only an opportunity for you to discuss what brings you to therapy but also to ask questions, decide if therapy is for you and whether you feel I am the right person for you and your child.

I offer short term (6-12 sessions) and open ended work depending on the young person’s needs, but will always work towards promoting self-reliance and independence.

I meet with parents of younger children periodically to review where we are in the process of supporting their child towards achieving their therapeutic goals. Adolescents may or may not feel that parental involvement in their therapy is helpful and thinking about this would form part of our work together.

Initial consultation appointments are usually 1 hour 15 minutes with a fee of £120. Ongoing therapy sessions are arranged at the same time and day each week and last 50 minutes. I offer daytime or after school sessions and work to school term dates (London Borough of Barnet). Sessions fees are £80, payable for parent meetings and regular appointments.
