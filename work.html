HOW I WORK


I believe that children and young people are constantly experiencing new challenges as they move through different developmental stages and at times this can be challenging not only for the young person but also for their parents and family. I aim to support children and young people within their family context; working with them whilst also supporting parents to help everyone navigate through the difficulties. I work to alleviate distress and promote emotional health in order to develop resilience and self-awareness so that young people can achieve their full personal, academic and social potential.

On meeting new patients, my main focus is helping children and their parents to feel at ease, so that current difficulties can be explored in a non-judgmental way. I sensitively address the child or young person’s symptoms and presenting issues whilst also exploring the family related needs and social factors (including educational, friendship, social media issues).

I understand that therapy can be a daunting prospect for all concerned and use a gentle, friendly, creative approach to help children and young people feel comfortable. Working in a flexible and adaptive way encourages the child or young person to express themselves in a way that feels right to them at the time, which can take the form of talking, play, games and creative arts.

Familiar issues that present are anxiety, stress, loss/bereavement, family breakdown, self-esteem, friendship issues, depression, transition, social anxiety and peer pressure. However, this list is not exhaustive and can mean different things in different contexts.
